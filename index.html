Military-Grade Encryption

Your todos are locked tighter than your ex's DMs.

AES-256-GCM encryption with PBKDF2 key derivation. We can't read your todos even if we wanted to—which we don't.

  • Zero-knowledge architecture: server stores only encrypted blobs
  • 100,000 PBKDF2 iterations. Good luck brute-forcing that.
  • Even if we get hacked, your data stays unreadable. Classifieds jealous.
  • Your password + our salt = your data, unhackable. Period.
💾

Offline-First Architecture

Works when your internet decides to take a vacation.

Your tasks persist locally via IndexedDB—no signal, no drama, no excuses.

  • IndexedDB primary storage: survives plane mode, blackouts, existential crises
  • localStorage backup: double insurance for paranoid users
  • Sync queue: offline changes auto-sync when you're back online
  • Your data follows you everywhere. Even to the middle of nowhere.
